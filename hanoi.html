Hanoi

Hanoi, the capital of Vietnam, is known for its beautiful architecture and is the second biggest city in the country. This is where most of the political events take place. Hanoi is full of history and the government passed bills to preserve many ancient architects. The food is rich, refreshing and well-prepared. My favorite dish was "Bun Ngang" which contains noodles, broth and duck meat. The streets are filled with small businesses and small restaurants. The northern Vietnamese people are incredibly smart and well-spoken.

Some DELICIOUS FOOD we had were Bun Ngang (noodles, broth and duck meat), different cakes, and desserts.

The stores in Hanoi are usually very narrow. You can find similar houses like this in Hong Kong too! Also don't forget to swing by the souvenir shops. Ao dai is the traditional Vietnamese dress. You can wear ao dai and a straw hat! That's usually the fit.

The next picture is "Ho Guom". It is the symbol of Hanoi. You can also see the ancient architects in this section.
